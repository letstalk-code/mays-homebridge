Mobile, Alabama is one of the most underrated housing markets in the Southeast. Affordable prices, strong neighborhoods, and a community that's growing — it's a great time to buy here. But knowing *where to start* is where most people get stuck.

Here's an honest, step-by-step breakdown of what buying a home in Mobile actually looks like.

## Step 1: Get Your Finances in Order

Before you look at a single listing, you need to know where you stand financially. That means:

- **Check your credit score.** Most conventional loans require a 620+. FHA loans can go lower, but the better your score, the better your rate.
- **Know your debt-to-income ratio.** Lenders look at how much of your monthly income goes to debt. Lower is better.
- **Start saving for your down payment and closing costs.** In Mobile, closing costs typically run 2–5% of the purchase price.

If your credit needs work, that's not a dead end — it's a starting point. I've helped clients go from "not ready" to "keys in hand" in less than a year with the right plan.

## Step 2: Get Pre-Approved (Not Just Pre-Qualified)

Pre-qualification is a quick estimate. Pre-approval is a real commitment from a lender based on your actual documents. In a competitive market, sellers take pre-approved buyers far more seriously.

Work with a lender who explains every option to you — not just the one that closes fastest.

## Step 3: Know What You're Looking For

Make a list of your must-haves and your nice-to-haves. Consider:

- Neighborhood and school district
- Commute distance
- Number of bedrooms and bathrooms
- Yard, garage, storage
- Age and condition of the home

Mobile has a range of neighborhoods — from Midtown to West Mobile to the Eastern Shore — each with its own character and price range. Knowing what matters to you makes the search much more focused.

## Step 5: Make an Offer and Negotiate

When you find the right home, your advocate will help you craft a competitive offer — one that protects you with the right contingencies while still being attractive to the seller.

In Mobile's current market, coming in with a clean, well-structured offer matters as much as the number itself.

## Step 6: Inspections, Appraisals, and Final Walkthrough

Never skip the inspection. A good inspector will give you a detailed picture of the home's condition, and that information is power in negotiations. If something's wrong, you can ask for repairs, a price reduction, or — if it's a dealbreaker — walk away.

## Step 7: Close

Closing day is when everything comes together. You'll sign documents, pay closing costs, and get your keys. Your advocate should be with you every step of this process to make sure you understand every line you sign.
