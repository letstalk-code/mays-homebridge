If you've served in the military, you've earned one of the most powerful homebuying benefits available — the VA loan. And if you're buying a home in Mobile, Alabama, it's one of the best tools you can use to get into a home with less out of pocket.

But a surprising number of veterans either don't know they qualify or don't fully understand how the program works. Let's fix that.

## What Is a VA Loan?

A VA loan is a mortgage backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs. The VA doesn't lend you the money directly — instead, they guarantee a portion of the loan, which reduces risk for lenders. That guarantee is what makes the benefits possible.

VA loans are offered through private lenders (banks, credit unions, mortgage companies), but they come with terms you won't find anywhere else.

## Who Qualifies?

You may be eligible if you are:

- An active-duty service member (with at least 90 days of service)
- A veteran with an honorable or general discharge
- A National Guard or Reserve member (with at least 6 years of service, or 90 days of active-duty deployment)
- A surviving spouse of a service member who died in the line of duty or from a service-connected disability

To confirm eligibility, you'll need a **Certificate of Eligibility (COE)** from the VA. Your lender can usually pull this for you electronically.

## Why VA Loans Are So Powerful

Here's what makes them stand out:

- **No down payment required.** You can finance 100% of the home's value. That alone saves most buyers $10,000–$30,000 upfront.
- **No private mortgage insurance (PMI).** Conventional loans charge PMI if you put less than 20% down. VA loans don't — ever.
- **Lower interest rates.** VA rates are typically 0.25%–0.5% lower than conventional rates.
- **Limited closing costs.** The VA restricts what fees lenders can charge, keeping your out-of-pocket costs down.
- **No prepayment penalty.** You can pay off your loan early without any fees.

For a first-time buyer in Mobile earning a median income, these benefits can mean the difference between affording a home and waiting years longer.

## What a VA Loan Doesn't Cover

There are a few things to know:

- **VA funding fee:** Most borrowers pay a one-time funding fee (typically 1.25%–3.3% of the loan amount). This can be rolled into the loan. Veterans with a service-connected disability are exempt.
- **Property requirements:** The home must meet VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs). These ensure the home is safe, structurally sound, and sanitary. Some fixer-uppers won't qualify.
- **Primary residence only:** VA loans are for homes you plan to live in — not investment properties or vacation homes.

## Common Myths About VA Loans

**"VA loans take too long to close."** Not true. With an experienced lender, VA loans close on the same timeline as conventional loans — typically 30–45 days.

**"Sellers don't like VA offers."** Some sellers worry about the VA appraisal being strict. But a well-structured offer from a qualified buyer is competitive regardless of loan type. This is where having an advocate matters.

**"You can only use a VA loan once."** Also false. VA loan entitlement can be restored and reused multiple times throughout your life.
