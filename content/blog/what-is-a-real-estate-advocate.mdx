You've heard of real estate agents. You've probably worked with one, or know someone who has. But have you ever heard of a real estate advocate?

There's a difference — and it's one that can cost or save you tens of thousands of dollars.

## What a Traditional Real Estate Agent Does

A traditional real estate agent is licensed to help you buy or sell a home. They're paid on commission — typically a percentage of the sale price. That means the higher the price, the more they make.

Here's what most people don't realize: in many transactions, the same agent represents both the buyer and the seller. That's called dual agency, and it puts your agent in a position where they can't fully fight for your interests without hurting the other party — who is also their client.

Nothing about that setup is designed to protect you.
